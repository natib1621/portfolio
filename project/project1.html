🛰 Geospatial Land Cover Classification & Temperature Change Detection

An internship machine learning project that analyzes satellite raster data of Addis Ababa across three years (2000, 2015 & 2025) to detect temperature change trends and classify land cover using both supervised and unsupervised ML techniques.

Project Overview

Geospatial data provides valuable insights into Earth's physical and environmental conditions. This internship project applies machine learning techniques to analyze multi-temporal satellite raster datasets of Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, spanning the years 2000, 2015, and 2025.

The project is divided into two complementary workflows: an Unsupervised Notebook for temperature change analysis using K-Means clustering, and a Supervised Notebook for land cover classification using Random Forest and XGBoost trained on labeled shapefiles.

Outputs include land cover maps showing how Addis Ababa's landscape has changed over 25 years — with clear evidence of urban expansion, deforestation, and rising surface temperatures — providing actionable data for policymakers, urban planners, and environmental agencies.

🔮 Methodology

  • Data: Satellite raster imagery (Landsat/Sentinel) for Addis Ababa — 2000, 2015 & 2025
  • Preprocessing: Raster data alignment, normalization, and band extraction
  • Unsupervised (K-Means Clustering): Grouped raster pixels to detect surface temperature anomalies and thermal trends over time
  • Supervised (Random Forest & XGBoost): Extracted raster values using training shapefiles, then trained ensemble models for multi-class land cover mapping
  • Output: Land cover classification maps for 2000, 2015, and 2025 highlighting urban growth, vegetation loss, and barren land expansion
  • Tools: Python, Jupyter Notebook, Scikit-learn, XGBoost, Geopandas, Rasterio, Matplotlib

📋 Key Objectives

  • Preprocess raster data for both land cover and temperature analysis
  • Implement unsupervised K-Means clustering to generate temperature change maps
  • Implement supervised Random Forest / XGBoost for multi-class land cover mapping
  • Compare the effectiveness of supervised vs. unsupervised approaches on geospatial raster data
  • Assess temperature change trends and land cover transitions from 2000 to 2025

💡 What I Learned

This internship project gave me hands-on experience with geospatial data analysis, raster preprocessing, and applying both supervised and unsupervised ML to real-world environmental datasets. I deepened my knowledge of Random Forest and XGBoost for classification, K-Means for clustering, and how to interpret and visualize land cover maps. I also gained valuable experience working in a structured, multi-chapter research document format typical of professional internship reporting.
